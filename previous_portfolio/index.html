Welcome!

I'm a learning engineer with experience in data science and experimental research, working to make learning more accessible and personalized.

About

I implemented several Machine Learning projects in both academic and professional settings, and I'm motivated by improving how people learn thanks to Technology. I am interested in any type of learning, with a preference for language. I specialized in Natural Language Processing algorithms that find themselves useful in all learning contexts (course material, questions/answers, language learning, etc.).

The two masters I graduate from: Master of Science and Engineering (June 2021) from CentraleSupelec, and the Master of Educational Technology and Applied Learning Science (August 2021) of Carnegie Mellon University give me a better understanding of the upsides and limitations of technology applied to learning.

My background in theoretical math and engineering provided me with the relevant skills to improve machine learning algorithms. I then focused on applying these skills to human learning at Carnegie Mellon University, at the intersection of computer science, instructional design and learning science. To illustrate, I designed an online course in Geography following evidence-based e-learning design principles. I worked as a data consultant in a research project at CMU, aiming at empowering low-resource Ivoirian teachers with an accessible chatbot.

I'm excited to apply my skills to improve existing learning solutions or design new ones.

Data Science

  • Data Scientist at SEME

Learning Engineering

  • Math ten-hour course for 2nd graders

    In a project at Carnegie Mellon as part of a two-person team, I designed a ten-hour math course for 2nd graders. Following backward design, we iteratively aligned goals, assessment, and instruction to the educational context, and created an evaluation research module for course improvement.

    Using a set of evidence-based 'Big Ideas' related to educational design best practices, I designed the entire course getting insights and supports from state school standards and a kindergarten teacher.

  • Personalized online learning module in Geography

    In a two-person team, I designed and implemented an online learning module for South-American geography learning. We tested a new way to learn geography by creating multiple connections among country's representations for learners. We included political, historical and social information, sometimes shared among multiple countries.

    From our theoretical approach to fast prototyping, we could apply Cognitive Task Analysis and A/B testing to refine our cognitive model and the module features. The cognitive model used is ACT-R (developed at Carnegie Mellon University), and we personalized instruction by implementing the production rules modeling students' understanding. We used the nools language also developed at Carnegie Mellon University.

    Cognitive Tutors, nools (javascript), Cognitive Task Analysis, A/B testing, e-learning design principles

  • Classes in disadvantaged schools for 7th graders, OSER

    I co-designed three-hour classes fostering open-mindedness for disadvantaged schools' 7th graders conducting classes in a team every other week for 6 months.

    Python, sklearn, seaborn, pandas
